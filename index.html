About Shanice

Welcome! My name is Shanice. I use she/they pronouns and identify as a queer neurodiverse Black woman. I'm a data-driven, people-first Software Engineer. I currently work at the ACLU on the Educate team, building out the user experiences and interactive components for the ACLU.org and supported websites.

I'm a CUNY graduate based in New York City, where I studied Emerging Media Technology and Game Design. In my free time, I enjoy volunteering in the tech community, learning new skills, watching musicals, and spending time on my Peloton bike.

As for my previous experience, I've had internships in front-end, back-end, quality assurance automation, and data analysis. I've collaborated with internal stakeholders to resolve key issues regarding automation, accessibility, developer experience, and data collection. This work ranges from the start-up level to multinational operations, such as NBCUniversal, Disney, Electronic Arts, and Twitter.

My main interests are in developer experience, front-end development, accessibility, internal tooling, and automation. However, I still identify as a generalist and enjoy a variety of technical roles across the tech stack. Transparency, frequent communication, and leading with empathy are the core values I follow in and out of the workplace.
